Policy
Should Common people be given the right to chose who should be the policymaker?

By Anirudha Kulkarni, Published on February 04th, 2021

I am afraid I have to disagree. I believe policymakers play an essential role in overall national development and should not be chosen by a common man. Consider a situation when a small kid got some disease and having stomach pain. What should you do? Should you go to an expert like a doctor or let that kid decide for himself? If he thinks eating chocolate will make him feel better even if he has diabetes and can die due to it? My claim is that policy is a much intellectually demanding thing; One cannot do this as time pass. One must have enough knowledge to comment on them.

A person who does his job most of the time and gains knowledge about what current government is doing or what leaders are doing from newspapers (with practically biased statistics or no data at all). Furthermore, he will be choosing who is going to make policy. So what do you expect to happen? In the end, the person who manages his Twitter profile well or can give a better speech and in the worst case, who can pay a lot wins the election.

We can change this. Let us consider a scenario when policymakers are elected by a group of experts from various fields, and those who are elected are also experts in the field. Who can evaluate the performance and understand the data. At this moment, half of the Indian population is against farm bills, and half is in against. Should there be a notion of subjectivity in this where data can clearly make a difference if the bill is against the farmer or in the favor? Even if there is subjectivity due to uncertainty in the future or lack of data, intellectual assumptions can be made and verified. There is no point in the current scenario. I tried my best to understand what the bill is saying and whether it favors or is against it. But I failed. It is not easy to claim on certain assumptions to claim to be valid or invalid. And that's where I was surprised. How are so many people on the roads claiming something about them? I do not doubt their intellect, but considering the current status of Indians in the global market, it's hard to believe any of them even understand them. I am not saying I understand them and make fun of others who are in favor or against them. The only thing I am saying is there are considerable people on both sides. Considerable of them must be wrong

And that's where I found the problem with the system. Why common people should be given the right to speak something upon which they don't understand. People just don't have enough resources and time to figure it. Half knowledge is dangerous than no knowledge.

Form an institution that is independent of everything. Make it private. Private institutions are better at handling corruption and unfairness compared to government. Give them enough rights to question everything and let them recruit some of the best in their respective fields. They will be the auditor of the government. Not common people. What common people can do is make a request to the auditors about the claim that they feel is inappropriate. Such an auditing system will audit every government policy, and everything government does. If it fails to achieve "Vikas," let the auditors who are intelligent enough to judge the effects can comment if its "acche din" or smokescreen by IT cell.

Conclusion

ordinary people should not be allowed to judge the performance of any policy or government. Preferably there should be an intellectual body of respected people from various institutions who can evaluate. We common people busy in everyday activities do not have enough resources and knowledge to do so.
